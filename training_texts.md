## Excerpt: Europäesch Wolleken  by [Jean Back](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jean_Back)

### Lëtzebuergesch (Original)

De Fliger ass d’Symbol fir eis Fräiheet. Heiansdo sëtzen ech am Gaart a kucken hinnen no, wéi se hir wäiss Sträifen um bloen Himmel hannerloossen.

Et ass eng Zort modernen Altor, op deem mir eis Dreem opferen, fir an d’Welt eraus ze fléien. Mä wat bleift vun deene Wolleken, wa mir erëm landen?

D’Welt ass méi kleng ginn, mä d’Distanz tëscht de Leit ass heiansdo méi grouss wéi jee virdrun. Mir fléien iwwer Grenzen ewech, mä d’Maueren an eise Käpp bleiwen dacks bestoen.

### English (Translation by google KI)

The airplane is the symbol of our freedom. Sometimes I sit in the garden and watch them, how they leave their white stripes on the blue sky.

It is a kind of modern altar on which we sacrifice our dreams to fly out into the world. But what remains of those clouds when we land again?

The world has become smaller, but the distance between people is sometimes greater than ever before. We fly across borders, but the walls in our heads often remain.

## National Anthem

### Lëtzebuergesch (Original)

Wou d'Uelzecht duurch d'Wisen zéit, duurch d'Fielsen d'Sauer brëcht,
Wou d'Rief laanscht d'Musel dofteg bléit, den Himmel Wein eis mécht:
Dat ass onst Land, fir dat mir géif hei nidden alles won,
Onst Heemechtsland, dat mir so déif an eisen Hierzer dron.
O Du do uewen, deem seng Hand duurch d'Welt d'Natioune leet,
Behitt du d'Lëtzebuerger Land vum frieme Joch a Leed!
Du hues on als Kand geperrelt, looss eis als Männer fräi,
An looss d'Sonn vun der Fräiheet liichten, fir d'éiweg an d'allerlee!

### English (Translation by google KI)

Where the Alzette flows through the meadows, the Sauer breaks through the rocks,
Where the vine fragrant blows along the Moselle, the sky makes wine for us:
That is our land, for which we would risk everything here below,
Our homeland, which we carry so deep in our hearts.
O Thou above, whose hand leads the nations through the world,
Protect the Luxembourgish land from foreign yoke and suffering!
You cherished us as children, let us be free as men,
And let the sun of freedom shine, for ever and always!

### English (Poetic Translation by Nicholas Weydert)

Where the Alzette slowly flows, The Sura plays wild pranks,
Where fragrant vineyards amply grow On the Mosella's banks;
There lies the land for which we would Dare everything down here,
Our own, our native land which ranks Deeply in our hearts. 
O Thou above whose powerful hand Makes States or lays them low,
Protect this Luxembourger land From foreign yoke and woe.
Your spirit of liberty bestow On us now as of yore.
Let Freedom's sun in glory glow For now and evermore.
